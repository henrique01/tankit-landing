Privacy policy

Your fuel data stays yours.

TankIt has no user account, no analytics SDK, and no tracking. Your receipt photos and fuel history stay on your device — and, if you turn on iCloud sync, in your own private iCloud. They are never sent to a TankIt server. The only thing TankIt sends out is your location, so it can fetch nearby fuel prices.

Short version: we physically can't see your data — there's no TankIt account or server that holds it.

01What stays on your devices

Everything you put into TankIt is stored in the app's local database on your device, using Apple's SwiftData framework. That includes:

  • Receipt photos you scan or import
  • Fuel entries (volume, price per litre, total, fuel type, date, station)
  • Odometer readings
  • Vehicle information you've entered
  • Your settings and preferences

Small pieces of data — like the latest cheapest-pump result — are shared between the main app and its widgets through an App Group container (group.com.tankit.shared) on your device. This container never leaves your iPhone either.

iCloud sync (optional)

If you turn on iCloud sync in Settings, your fuel entries, vehicles and receipt photos are kept in step across your own Apple devices through your private iCloud account, using Apple's CloudKit. That data lives in your iCloud — governed by Apple's privacy policy — and TankIt has no way to read it. There is no TankIt account and no TankIt server anywhere in the sync path: it goes from your device, to your iCloud, to your other devices. Sync is optional; leave it off and everything stays on the single device.

02What leaves your device

When you open the price finder, TankIt sends your location to look up nearby fuel prices. Depending on the country, the request goes to one of three places:

  • Austria: directly to the federal energy regulator (api.e-control.at).
  • France: directly to the open government fuel-price dataset (data.economie.gouv.fr).
  • Australia (NSW & TAS): through a small TankIt-operated proxy (a Cloudflare Worker) that forwards your location to the official Australian government price feeds and returns the results.

We send your GPS coordinate so the feed can return stations near you. We do not send any user identifier, advertising ID, account, or anything that would identify you. For caching and to keep request volume low, lookups are grouped to a roughly one-kilometre grid.

Why the Australian proxy exists: the Australian feeds require API credentials that can't be safely shipped inside an app, so those requests pass through our proxy instead of going direct. The proxy only relays the price lookup — it never receives your fuel entries, receipts, or identity, and it is not an analytics or tracking endpoint. To stop automated abuse of that endpoint, each Australian request carries a one-time Apple App Attest assertion proving it came from a genuine, unmodified copy of TankIt. App Attest confirms app integrity with Apple; it is not an advertising identifier and cannot be used to identify you.

Apart from these price lookups (and iCloud sync, if you enable it), TankIt has no backend: no analytics endpoint, no telemetry collector, and no TankIt server that ever receives your fuel data, receipts, or identity.

03Permissions

The app asks for two iOS permissions, both optional:

  • Camera — to scan fuel receipts. Usage string: "Tank It uses the camera to scan fuel receipts." You can still use the app without granting camera access, by entering fills manually or importing a photo via the share extension.
  • Location (when in use) — to find nearby fuel stations. Usage string: "Tank It uses your location to find nearby fuel stations." Without location access the price finder won't work, but every other feature does.

Both permissions can be revoked at any time in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security.

04Apple Privacy Manifest

TankIt ships a Privacy Manifest (PrivacyInfo.xcprivacy) declaring exactly which Apple "required-reason" APIs the app touches. All of them are standard iOS plumbing, not data collection:

  • User Defaults (reason CA92.1) — storing app settings on your device.
  • File timestamps (reason C617.1) — reading the dates of files you provide.
  • Disk space (reason E174.1) — checking that there's room to save a receipt photo.
  • System boot time (reason 35F9.1) — calculating local elapsed time inside the app.

The manifest declares NSPrivacyTracking as false, with no tracking domains and no collected data types.

05No tracking, no ads

TankIt has no analytics SDK, no crash reporter that phones home, no advertising identifier, no third-party trackers of any kind. We do not use cookies because there is no website session, no account, and no server to log in to.

We don't show ads. We never sell or share your data, because we don't have it.

06Children

TankIt isn't directed at children under 13. It doesn't knowingly collect any information from anyone, so it doesn't knowingly collect information from children either.

07Export & deletion

Your data is portable. From the Settings screen inside the app you can export:

  • A CSV file with every fill-up.
  • A ZIP archive bundling every receipt photo plus the CSV.

To erase everything, use Settings → Delete All Data inside the app. That clears the local database and the App Group container, and — if iCloud sync is on — removes the synced copy from your iCloud too.

Note that with iCloud sync enabled, simply deleting the app does not remove your data from iCloud — it would sync back if you reinstall. To wipe it, run Delete All Data first, or turn off iCloud sync before deleting the app.

08Changes to this policy

If we ever change how the app handles data, we'll update this page and bump the effective date below. Material changes will also be flagged in the app's release notes.
