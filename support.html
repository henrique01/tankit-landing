01Getting started

Once TankIt is installed on your iPhone, the first run takes about a minute:

  1. Open the app — no account, no sign-in.
  2. Grant camera access when prompted, so you can scan receipts.
  3. Grant location access (while using the app), so the price finder can show stations near you.
  4. Scan your first receipt: hit the camera button, fill the frame with the paper, hold steady. The app finds the receipt edges and auto-corrects perspective.
  5. Review the extracted fields (volume, total, price per litre, station, date), correct anything that's off, save.

Both permissions are optional — you can still enter fills manually and search stations by address if you'd rather not grant them.

02Scanning receipts

The scan didn't pick up the right numbers

Every extracted field is editable on the confirm screen. Tap the value, fix it, save. The original photo stays attached to the entry, so you can always re-open and check.

The whole receipt didn't scan

Try again with even, diffuse light and no shadow across the printed area. Crumpled paper is fine; folds across the totals line are harder. If the receipt is thermal-paper faded, manual entry is faster than fighting the OCR.

I already have a photo of the receipt

From any app that handles photos (Photos, Files, Messages, Mail), use the iOS share sheet and pick "Scan with Tankit". The app opens with the photo pre-loaded.

Re-cropping after the fact

From the fill history, open an entry and tap the receipt photo — you can re-run the rectangle detection and perspective correction at any time.

03Price finder

The list is empty

Two things to check first:

  • Location services are on for TankIt (iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Location Services → TankIt → "While Using the App").
  • You're in a supported country. TankIt covers Austria, France, and Australia (NSW & TAS). Other countries will return no results until we add them.

The cheapest station I see in the app and on its sign don't match

TankIt shows the prices that the operator submitted to the national registry. Pump prices can change between updates, and some stations post slower than others. The government feeds are the source of truth — if a station's pricing isn't current, it's an issue with their reporting.

I tapped "navigate" but nothing happened

TankIt opens Apple Maps for directions. Make sure Apple Maps is installed (it's a system app and can't normally be removed, but on some managed devices it is).

04CarPlay & widgets

TankIt doesn't appear on my CarPlay screen

In CarPlay, tap the app grid and look for TankIt. If it's missing, go to iOS Settings → General → CarPlay → [your car] → Customize, and drag TankIt into the visible apps list.

The widget shows old data

Widgets refresh on iOS's schedule, but they're seeded fresh every time you open the app. If a widget feels stale, opening TankIt once will update it. Widgets also rely on having recent location data — using the app or the price finder near where you want the widget to be accurate is the fastest way to refresh.

05Your data

Export

From the app's Settings screen you can export:

  • A CSV file with every fill-up.
  • A ZIP archive containing the CSV plus every receipt photo.

You can share the file through the iOS share sheet — AirDrop it to your Mac, save to Files, email it, whatever fits.

iCloud sync

Turn on iCloud sync in Settings to keep your fill-ups, vehicles and receipt photos in step across your own Apple devices. Sync runs through your private iCloud account (Apple CloudKit) — there's no TankIt account or server involved, and we can't see any of it. Leave it off and everything stays on the one device.

Backup

Your TankIt data is backed up wherever your iPhone is backed up — iCloud or your computer. Restoring an iPhone backup brings TankIt's data back with it.

Deletion

To erase everything, use Settings → Delete All Data in the app — that clears the local database, the App Group container, and (if sync is on) the copy in your iCloud. With iCloud sync enabled, just deleting the app won't remove the iCloud copy: it would sync back on reinstall, so run Delete All Data or turn sync off first.

06FAQ

Which countries does TankIt support?

It depends on the feature:

  • Receipt scanner — works with receipts in English, German and French, from stations anywhere. The on-device OCR is tuned for those three languages, so a receipt from Italy or Spain will still scan but field detection is less reliable.
  • Cheapest-pump finder — live prices cover Austria, France, and Australia (NSW & TAS), because each country requires wiring up its official government fuel-price feed. More countries are planned as we add feeds.

So you can track fill-ups anywhere; you just won't get nearby-price suggestions outside the supported countries yet.

Is there an Android version?

No. TankIt is iPhone only. A lot of what makes it good — on-device receipt parsing, CarPlay, native widgets, Apple Maps integration — leans on Apple-only frameworks.

Do I need an account?

No. There is no TankIt sign-in, no email collection, nothing to remember — open the app and use it. (Optional iCloud sync uses your own Apple Account, not a TankIt account.)

Does it work offline?

Yes — receipt scanning, entry, and statistics work fully offline. The price finder needs a network connection to query the government feeds.

What iOS version do I need?

iOS 18 or later, iPhone only.

Does the app track me or sell my data?

No. There are no analytics SDKs, no advertising IDs, no third-party trackers, and no TankIt account. The only network calls send your location to look up fuel prices — straight to the Austrian and French government feeds, or via a small TankIt proxy for Australia. See the privacy policy for the full picture.

Can I track more than one car?

Yes. Your first vehicle is free; tracking additional vehicles is a Premium feature. Each vehicle keeps its own history, efficiency and stats, and you can switch between them from the header on the Home, History and Insights screens.

How does it handle partial fills?

Properly. A common bug in fuel apps is that a partial fill breaks the L/100 km calculation for that period. TankIt accumulates volumes across consecutive partial fills and only closes the efficiency cycle at the next full fill.

I found a bug / I have a feature idea.

Excellent — email tankit@tankit.app with as much detail as you can. Screenshots help, version number helps, and so does "what I was doing right before it happened."
